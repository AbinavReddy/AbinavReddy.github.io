San Francisco Crime: A COVID-Era Data Story

Exploring how five major crime categories shifted across the city's police districts before, during, and after the pandemic (2003 – 2024).

1. Twenty Years of Crime at a Glance

The line chart below tracks yearly incident counts for Assault, Burglary, Larceny/Theft, Robbery and Vehicle Theft from 2003 to 2024. The shaded band marks the COVID-19 period (2020–2021), where some categories dipped while others surged.

2. Burglary Hotspots: Pre-COVID vs. During COVID

Toggle between the two layers on the map to compare where burglaries concentrated before the pandemic (2017–2019, blue) versus during it (2020–2021, red). Notice how hotspots shifted away from commercial corridors and into residential areas.

3. Crime Shift by District

The interactive bar chart compares average yearly incidents per district across three periods: Pre-COVID (2017–19), COVID (2020–21), and Post-COVID (2022–24). Click legend items to isolate individual crime types.

Data source: San Francisco Police Department incident reports (2003–2025). Built for DTU 02806 Social Data Analysis & Visualization.
